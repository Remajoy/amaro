What is SDG 5?

SDG 5 (Sustainable Development Goal 5) focuses on achieving gender equality and empowering all women and girls. It aims to remove barriers that prevent women from reaching their full potential.

Why SDG 5 Matters

  • Gender equality improves economies and strengthens communities.
  • Millions of women still experience violence and discrimination.
  • Women do more unpaid work than men worldwide.
  • In many countries, women still lack equal access to education and leadership roles.

What SDG 5 Aims to Achieve

  • End all forms of violence against women and girls.
  • Ensure equal participation in leadership and decision-making.
  • Promote equal rights to education, healthcare, and opportunities.
  • Eliminate harmful practices like early and forced marriage.
  • Guarantee reproductive health and rights for all women.
