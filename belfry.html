Welcome to Jaro Belfry

A historic bell tower and one of the most iconic landmarks of Jaro, Iloilo City. Standing tall across the Jaro Cathedral, the Belfry is a proud reminder of Iloilo’s deep-rooted faith and colonial past.

About the Jaro Belfry

The Jaro Belfry, also known as the “Campanario de Jaro,” was built in 1744 and stands as a symbol of faith and resilience. Once serving as a watchtower against pirate invasions, it now remains one of Iloilo’s most visited heritage sites.

Historical Background

1744 - Construction

The Jaro Belfry was built as a watchtower and bell tower, serving as a symbol of faith and protection against pirates.

1948 - Earthquake Damage

The Belfry suffered significant damage but remained a strong symbol of resilience for the local community.

1990 - Restoration

Restored carefully to preserve its original structure and design, ensuring its place as a historic landmark.

Today

It stands proudly beside the cathedral, drawing tourists and locals who admire its history and beauty.
