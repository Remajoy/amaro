Jaro Belfry & Cathedral

In the heart of Jaro, Iloilo City, stand two of the most treasured landmarks of Western Visayas — the Jaro Cathedral and the Jaro Belfry. These magnificent structures embody centuries of devotion, culture, and Spanish colonial architecture that shaped the city's identity.

The Jaro Cathedral, also known as the National Shrine of Our Lady of the Candles, was built in 1864 and is one of the few churches in the Philippines where the bell tower stands separately across the street — the iconic Jaro Belfry. Both serve as lasting reminders of Iloilo’s faith and resilience through time.

1. Jaro Cathedral

Marvel at its Romanesque Revival design, elegant facade, and the venerated image of Nuestra Señora de la Candelaria, the patroness of Western Visayas.

2. Jaro Belfry

This 18th-century belfry once served as a watchtower against invaders and now stands as a silent witness to Jaro’s vibrant past.

3. Jaro Plaza

Surrounding the landmarks is the charming Jaro Plaza — a peaceful spot where heritage meets the local rhythm of life.

4. Cultural Heritage

Every February, the Jaro Fiesta comes alive, honoring the Lady of the Candles with grand processions and heartfelt devotion.
