About Jaro Cathedral

The Jaro Cathedral (Church of St. Elizabeth of Hungary) was built in 1864, when the district became a diocese by order of Pope Pius IX. Destroyed in the quake of January 1948 and restored by Archbishop Jose Ma. Cuenco in 1956, its style is mainly Baroque, with Gothic elements from renovations.

Historical Background

1864 - Diocese Established

The first structure of Jaro Cathedral was erected under Bishop Mariano Cuartero. The district became a diocese by order of Pope Pius IX.

1948 - Earthquake Damage

The cathedral suffered destruction during the 1948 quake but was restored to preserve its heritage and architectural charm.

1956 - Restoration

Restored under Archbishop Jose Ma. Cuenco, combining Baroque style with Gothic elements added during renovations.

1981 - Papal Visit

Our Lady of the Candles was personally crowned by Pope John Paul II, highlighting the cathedral’s significance as a pilgrimage site.
