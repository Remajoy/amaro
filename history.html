Major Milestones in the Fight for Gender Equality

1848: Seneca Falls Convention

The first women’s rights convention in the U.S., starting the organized fight for equality.

1920: Women's Suffrage

The 19th Amendment granted women the right to vote after decades of activism.

1979: CEDAW Adopted

The UN adopted a global treaty protecting women’s rights and banning discrimination.

1995: Beijing Platform for Action

Countries committed to a historic plan for advancing global gender equality.

2015: SDG 5 Established

SDG 5 became a global target focused on equal opportunities, safety, and rights.
